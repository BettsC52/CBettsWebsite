Hi, I am Charles Betts

In 2018 I graduated in BEng Mechanical Engineering and subsequently enrolled for the new MSc Industrial Digitalisation based at Manchester Metropolitan University’s PrintCity, graduating in 2020 with a Merit.

I always had a passion for understanding mechanical assemblies and how they work since I was young, and through the bachelor's degree, I gained an appreciation for CAD and simulations, creating scenarios and investigating the effects of both FEA and CFD.

With additive manufacturing gaining traction I applied and was accepted to be a part of the MSc Industrial Digitalisation degree. This allowed me to continue learning and developing my interests in FEA and CFD while learning manufacturing techniques. Gaining an understanding in not only design for additive manufacturing techniques including FDM and SLA, but the benefits both new and traditional techniques bring.

My year-long project, titled “Part Consolidation of Multi-Component Assemblies” focussed on an assembly, in my instance a baby stroller, and produced a methodology of how to reduce the number of parts to produce an assembly with the same or better characteristics. The research also investigated the benefits both to product life cycle and sustainability. I believe it is important to understand the environmental impact and financial savings to both business and consumers.
